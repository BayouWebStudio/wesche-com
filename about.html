The work

I've been tattooing geometric and sacred geometry for over a decade. Large-scale pieces — full sleeves, back pieces, chest panels. The kind of work that takes a full day and requires both of us to show up ready.

I specialize in sacred geometry — Platonic solids, mandalas, the Flower of Life, Metatron's Cube. Patterns that have existed for thousands of years and still feel like they're pointing at something real. I don't know exactly what, but that's part of why I keep making them.

I work out of Houston, TX at Tattoo Temple Houston. People fly in from all over — which still surprises me, honestly. I just try to do the work as well as I can.

The painting

A few years ago I started painting on canvas. Same obsession, different surface. I wanted to see what the geometry looked like when it wasn't constrained by skin — when it could be as large as I wanted, as detailed as I wanted, without a client having to sit through it.

The studio closes in March 2027. After that I'm moving toward painting full time. This page is part of that transition — building something that outlasts any single tattoo.

The community

I run @omfgeometry on Instagram and the Geometric Tattoos Facebook group. Not for business reasons — just because I genuinely love this style and wanted a place where it could be celebrated properly. There are artists doing incredible geometric work all over the world who deserve more eyes on them.
